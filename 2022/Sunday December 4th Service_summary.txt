• The season of Advent is a time to wait for Jesus' second coming and is marked by reflection on his first coming
• Isaiah 9 contains prophecies about the coming King who would be the Wonderful Counselor, Mighty God, Everlasting Father, and Prince of Peace
• Christ as the Mighty God is explored, with reference to Isaiah's prophecy and its scandalous implications for Jewish and Western cultures
• The story of Joe Lewis, a professional boxer, is used to illustrate the power and might of Jesus
• Isaiah's prophecies are seen as audacious and scandalous, claiming that a child can be described in terms typically reserved for God alone
• Isaiah prophesies about a coming child who will bring peace and justice to Israel
• The people of Israel are worried and fearful about war and their future
• Isaiah tells them that God has not forgotten them and will accomplish His purposes in His time
• The people must learn how to wait on God and trust in His sovereignty
• A child, Jesus, is born centuries later who embodies the qualities described by Isaiah: a wonderful counselor, mighty God, everlasting Father, and Prince of Peace
• Jesus demonstrates His power as the mighty God through various miracles and healings.
• A miracle story from Mark chapter 2 about a paralyzed man lowered through the roof for healing
• Jesus forgives the man's sins and tells him to take up his mat and walk
• Jesus' power to forgive sin is contrasted with his ability to heal physical bodies
• The Incarnation of God in Christ, as described by Dietrich Bonhoeffer
• Isaiah prophecies about a coming child who will reign in peace and also suffer for humanity's sins
• The connection between the cradle (Jesus' birth) and the cross (Jesus' crucifixion)
• Jesus' ultimate confrontation with sin, death, and Satan
• Jesus' victory over death, sin, and Satan through the cross
• Participation in Christ's resurrection life and victory
• Advent waiting for Christ's second coming to fully eradicate all evil
• The already-not yet tension of experiencing partial victory while still living with sickness, pain, and misery
• Waiting in hope for a warrior king who will fully establish God's kingdom on earth
• Celebration of the fullness of Jesus' victory when He returns
