• The word "gospel" is used frequently in Christian literature and preaching.
• In the New Testament, the word "gospel" refers to the good news of salvation through Jesus Christ.
• The concept of gospel as good news is rooted in ancient Roman culture, where it was used to announce victories or births.
• According to Paul in 1 Corinthians 15, the gospel is centred on Jesus' death, burial, and resurrection.
• Understanding the gospel is essential for the Christian faith.
• The Christian faith and teaching collapses if Jesus did not rise from the dead
• Paul emphasizes that the gospel is about events, not just ideas or teachings
• These events, such as Jesus' resurrection, are witnessed to by the apostles
• The story of Jesus makes sense of these events, giving them significance and meaning
• Without the context of the larger biblical narrative, the individual events do not have much meaning

• The story of the Bible starts with a garden and ends with a new heaven and earth.
• Genesis describes humanity's rebellion against God and their decision to "do it on our own".
• The Old Testament narrates the catastrophic slide into sin, and the spiralling out of control caused by human rebellion.
• The Tower of Babel is seen as a repeat of the original sin in Genesis 3.
• From Genesis 12 to Malachi, God rolls up his sleeves to deal with the consequences of sin.
• God rescues and redeems a people to himself, giving them his presence, protection, and instruction on how to live.
• The story doesn't end with a static state of enjoyment, but rather an endless cycle of rebellion against God.
• The story of Malachi sets the stage for the arrival of Jesus
• Jesus' public ministry begins with a message of good news and repentance
• The kingdom of God is established through Jesus' life, death, and resurrection
• Old Testament promises are fulfilled in Jesus, including:

• A story about a pastor who explained Christianity to a barber
• A barber and a pastor have a conversation about what kind of world they want to live in.
• The barber expresses strong convictions about environmental justice and equality.
• The pastor shares that he wants to live in a world where there's no waste or pollution and where the marginalized are treated justly.
• He explains that this is possible through the death and resurrection of Jesus, who has secured a new creation.
